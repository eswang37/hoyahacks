Everything you need to know before, during, and after HoyaHacks 2027. If something's missing, just email us.

Registration & Eligibility
HoyaHacks is open to all college and university students, as well as high school students. You don't need to be a Georgetown student — we welcome hackers from any school. Beginners and first-timers are especially encouraged to apply!
Register through one of the two forms on our registration page:
  • College participant form
  • High school participant form
You'll also need to submit your final project on Devpost by the deadline.
Nope! HoyaHacks is completely free to attend. Meals, snacks, and swag are all included. We're able to do this thanks to our generous sponsors.
Teams can be 1–4 people. You can register solo and find teammates at our team formation event on Friday evening — we'll help make sure no one is left out. Teams can also be a mix of college and high school participants.
HoyaHacks 2027 is an in-person event at Georgetown University. We do not offer a remote participation option this year. If travel is a concern, reach out to us — we may be able to offer limited travel stipends.
Logistics & Travel
The event is held in the McDonough Gymnasium / Armory complex at Georgetown University, 37th and O Street NW, Washington, D.C. Exact room assignments will be sent closer to the event. Check-in opens at the Armory Entrance.
We offer limited travel reimbursements for participants traveling from outside the DC area. If you need travel assistance, please note it in your registration form. We'll do our best to accommodate as many applicants as possible.
The hackathon runs overnight from Friday to Sunday, and you're welcome to stay on-site throughout. We provide a dedicated sleep area, but it's minimal — most hackers just work through the night. We don't arrange hotel accommodations, but can share nearby hotel recommendations upon request.
  • Your laptop and charger
  • Student ID
  • A change of clothes and toiletries if you're staying overnight
  • Sleeping bag or blanket (optional, if you plan to nap)
  • Your best ideas 💡
We provide all meals, snacks, and beverages throughout the event.
Hacking & Projects
All hacking must start at the official kickoff at 7:30 PM on Friday Jan 22. You may brainstorm ideas and form teams beforehand, but writing code, designing prototypes, or building any components before the start time is not allowed.

Rules & Code of Conduct
HoyaHacks follows the MLH Code of Conduct. All participants, mentors, sponsors, and volunteers are expected to uphold a respectful, inclusive environment. Violations will result in immediate removal from the event.
Yes! HoyaHacks 2027 is an official event in the Major League Hacking 2027 season. MLH provides additional resources, hardware, and mentors on the day of the event.
